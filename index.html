About Nancy

I am an American-German midwife and researcher who has lived and worked in Germany for nearly forty years. My path into midwifery began with a deep curiosity about birth as both a biological and cultural phenomenon.

After qualifying as a midwife in 2001, I practised in a wide range of settings, including hospital delivery wards and a free-standing birth centre. I currently work in a hospital-based midwifery team, where I continue to combine hands-on practice with a reflective, research-informed approach to care.

Alongside my clinical work, I have always been drawn to questions of how knowledge is created, shared, and embodied in midwifery. I studied sociology (B.A.) and public health (MscPH) before completing my PhD in Community Health and Midwifery at the University of Central Lancashire in 2019.

Becoming a Midwife in a Free-standing Birth Centre

"I am the midwife I dreamed of becoming"

Available: Pre-order January 8, 2026

Ships: After January 29, 2026

This book explores how newly qualified midwives acquire the practical skills and confidence that shape their early professional identities. Drawing from extensive research across German birth centres, it reveals how learning in midwifery is far more than skill acquisition—it is a process of becoming, reflection, and shared experience between generations of midwives.

For Professionals:

Essential reading for student midwives, newly qualified midwives, and midwifery educators seeking insights into skill development and professional identity formation.

For Mothers & Birth Advocates:

Offers valuable understanding of how midwives develop their expertise and the philosophy behind midwifery care, helping families make informed decisions about their birth experiences.

Research Focus

ASK a Midwife Study (2021-2024)

Led the postdoctoral study "Acquisition of Skills and Knowledge of Midwives," funded by the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research. Through more than seventy visits to birth centres across Germany, explored how newly qualified midwives develop their professional identities.

Lived Experiences in Birth

Doctoral research explored birth in free-standing birth centres, highlighting how women's and midwives' lived experiences of pregnancy, labour, and postpartum care offer vital insights into physiological birth and safe practice.

Knowledge Transmission

Committed to understanding how knowledge is created, shared, and embodied in midwifery, with particular focus on the transmission of wisdom between generations of midwives.

My writing, both academic and reflective, often draws inspiration from literature, mythology, and narrative, viewing birth as a profoundly human encounter that resists simplification. I am committed to the transmission of knowledge between generations of midwives and am guided by the conviction that each birth invites us to always approach care as an interaction rather than a procedure.
